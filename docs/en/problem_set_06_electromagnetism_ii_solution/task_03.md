## Problem 3 — Magnetic moment of a loop

### 1. Magnetic moment $\vec{\mu}$

For a loop of $N$ turns, area $S$, current $I$:

$$
\boxed{\vec{\mu} = N\,I\,S\,\hat{n}}
$$

where $\hat{n}$ is the unit normal to the loop, oriented by the right-hand rule with respect to $I$.

### 2. Torque $\vec{M}$

$$
\vec{M} = \vec{\mu}\times\vec{B}
$$

Magnitude: $M = \mu B\sin\theta = NISB\sin\theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between $\vec{\mu}$ and $\vec{B}$.

### 3. Maximum torque

$$
\sin\theta = 1 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \theta = 90^\circ
$$

i.e., when $\vec{\mu}\perp\vec{B}$ (the loop's plane is **parallel** to $\vec{B}$).

### 4. Potential energy

$$
\boxed{U = -\vec{\mu}\cdot\vec{B} = -\mu B\cos\theta}
$$

### 5. Stable and unstable positions

- **Stable equilibrium** ($U$ minimum): $\theta=0$, i.e., $\vec{\mu}\parallel\vec{B}$. Then $U=-\mu B$.
- **Unstable equilibrium** ($U$ maximum): $\theta=\pi$, i.e., $\vec{\mu}$ antiparallel to $\vec{B}$. Then $U=+\mu B$.
